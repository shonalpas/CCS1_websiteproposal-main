History of International State College of the Philippines

ISCP Founder

In the hallowed annals of educational history, where logic takes a backseat and exaggeration sits proudly in the driver's seat, we find the grand tale of Dr. Nickita Marahilig Dominguez Y Alfonso Ramirez, the visionary time- traveling education enthusiast who apparently founded the International State College of the Philippines in the distant past of 1596. Yes, dear readers, you read it right; she managed to establish a cutting-edge institution in an era where quills were mightier than swords.

With a heart brimming with noble intentions and perhaps a sprinkle of magic, Dr. Nickita embarked on a construction frenzy, miraculously raising the main campus from the ground up in a mere month. The workers, fueled by an otherworldly dedication, came together to create what can only be described as the best campus in the entire universeâ€”move over, Hogwarts! As time trotted along, Dr. Nickita's ambitions grew, and she proposed the creation of satellite campuses, because, why stop at just one impossible feat? Today, the International State College of the Philippines stands tall as a beacon of education, transforming unsuspecting students into superior beings, as if education there comes with a secret potion for enlightenment.

The current head of this illustrious institution, with a firm grip on the imaginary torch passed down from our time-traveling founder, valiantly vows to continue this legacy. Because, after all, where else can the current generation experience the unique blend of historical fantasy and educational enlightenment that only this school, and apparently a time-traveling founder, can provide?




ISCP Campus

ISCP Main Campus '1596

Spanish Collonial Era

In the mythical land of fantastical foundations and academic absurdity, the International State College of the Philippines (ISCP) main campus stands proudly as a testament to historical hocus-pocus and architectural alchemy. Legend has it that the campus was constructed overnight by a team of magical construction wizards armed with enchanted blueprints and bottomless coffee mugs. The campus, which is rumored to have been built on the back of a friendly neighborhood dragon, boasts towers that touch the sky and classrooms with ceilings so high that students occasionally need binoculars to spot their professors.

The campus library, an ancient tome-filled sanctuary, is said to house every book ever written, including unpublished works by Shakespeare and love letters penned by Cleopatra herself. It's whispered among students that the library's magical librarian can recite the Dewey Decimal System backward in her sleep and has the uncanny ability to summon books with just a flick of her wand.

The classrooms, rumored to be enchanted with mind-enhancing spells, allegedly imbue students with instant knowledge upon entering. Professors, or rather, sorcerers of wisdom, are said to have mastered the art of teaching via telepathy, making lectures obsolete and textbooks nothing more than decorative paperweights. The campus grounds, rumored to be enchanted with a perpetual spring, bloom with flowers that can recite Shakespearean sonnets and dance to Beethoven's symphonies. Students often find themselves engaged in heated debates with squirrels that quote Nietzsche and rabbits that discuss quantum physics.

In this whimsical world of academic enchantment, the International State College of the Philippines main campus stands as a beacon of magical education, where reality takes a back seat, and imagination holds the steering wheel. Who needs history when you have folklore this fantastic?


ISCP Biringan Campus '1598

In the enchanted realm of whimsy and academic trickery, the International State College of the Philippines Biringan Campus emerged mysteriously in the year 1598. According to local legends, the campus was summoned into existence during a lunar eclipse, as a group of mystical scholars chanted incantations, waved diplomas instead of wands, and brewed a potion made of crushed textbooks and phoenix feathers. Legend has it that the campus appeared in a puff of glittering smoke, nestled between dimensions, accessible only to those with a penchant for peculiar portals.

Upon arrival, students were greeted by talking trees that dispensed academic advice and squirrels proficient in calculus. The curriculum, shrouded in secrecy, reportedly included courses like "Transfiguration and Turning Professors into Pumpkins 101" and "Advanced Potion Brewing for Exams Stress Relief." Professors were said to possess magical powers, capable of conjuring A grades out of thin air and transforming failing grades into flying unicorns. Biringan Campus was renowned for its extracurricular activities, including broomstick races, invisibility cloak fashion shows, and a peculiar game called "Wand Dueling Chess," where chess pieces engaged in wizard duels to capture opponents' pawns. The campus also boasted a Quidditch team of flying llamas, which became the stuff of local legend and the subject of many fantastical tales.

However, Biringan Campus was not without its challenges. Students occasionally got lost in the labyrinthine hallways, finding themselves in alternate dimensions where math problems solved themselves and essays were composed by enchanted quills. Rumors persisted of a mischievous poltergeist that would rearrange students' notes into nonsensical poetry and turn lecture halls into dance floors during exams.


ISCP Moon Campus '1636

Twin Campus of ISCP-Sun Campus

In the peculiar chronicles of academic lunacy, the International State College of the Philippines Moon Campus, affectionately known as the "Lunar Learning Lair," made its grand debut in the lunar year of 1636. This ethereal institution, often touted as the twin sibling of its sun-soaked counterpart, the ISCP-Sun Campus, was the brainchild of a group of ambitious stargazers and eccentric astronomers who believed that education was not just for Earthlings but for moon-dwellers too.

Legend has it that the campus was constructed using moon dust, bound together with a special adhesive made from crushed meteorites and unicorn tears, ensuring a celestial ambiance that left students and professors alike in a perpetual state of starry-eyed wonder. The classrooms, equipped with anti-gravity desks and holographic blackboards, floated gently in the lunar atmosphere, providing an out-of-this-world learning experience. The Moon Campus curriculum was a blend of earthly subjects and extraterrestrial studies. Students were offered courses like "Astrobiology and Alien Etiquette," where they learned how to communicate with intergalactic neighbors and decipher extraterrestrial emojis. "Lunar Landscaping and Crater Architecture" was a popular major, teaching students how to design moon bases that were both stylish and meteor-resistant.

Of course, challenges arose in this lunar utopia. Moon Campus students occasionally had to deal with pesky moon dust allergies and the inconvenience of wearing spacesuits to class, but these minor inconveniences were considered a small price to pay for the privilege of receiving an education in the cosmic cradle of knowledge.


ISCP Sun Campus '1636

Twin Campus of ISCP-Moon Campus

In the whimsical tapestry of educational eccentricity, the International State College of the Philippines Sun Campus, born under the blazing sun in 1636, emerged as the radiant twin of its lunar counterpart, the ISCP-Moon Campus. Founded by a group of visionary sun-worshippers and solar enthusiasts, this campus was envisioned as a temple of enlightenment where learning was as scorching as the tropical sun overhead.

The curriculum at the Sun Campus was as diverse as the sun's rays. Students delved into subjects like "Solar Engineering and Sunglasses Design," exploring the art of harnessing solar energy while looking stylish. "Tropical Tan Studies and SPF Application Techniques" was a popular major, teaching students the science behind the perfect beach tan and how to avoid sunburns during midday siestas. Extracurricular activities under the Sun Campus's golden glow were nothing short of sizzling. The Sunbathing Club organized competitions for the most creative sun hat designs, while the Solar Surfing Team rode metaphorical waves of knowledge, exploring the mysteries of sunspots and solar flares.

Of course, challenges arose in this sun-soaked paradise. Students occasionally suffered from sun-induced daydreaming, staring at the sunbeams filtering through palm leaves instead of paying attention in class. Yet, these minor distractions were considered a small price to pay for the privilege of receiving an education under the perpetual sunshine of the International State College of the Philippines Sun Campus.


ISCP Galactic Campus '1658

In the galactic year 1658, the International State College of the Philippines (ISCP) Galactic Campus was founded with grand ambitions to educate beings from across the universe. However, due to a clerical error, the campus was built on a remote asteroid, light-years away from any civilization.

Despite this setback, the ISCP Galactic Campus became renowned for its avant-garde approach to education. Professors taught classes on intergalactic knitting and quantum cooking, while students majored in obscure subjects like Alien Etiquette and Starship Repair 101. The campus mascot, a sentient nebula named Nebby, was adored by all. Surprisingly, the ISCP Galactic Campus gained interstellar fame for its annual "Intergalactic Absurdity Olympics," where participants competed in bizarre events such as zero-gravity poetry slams and teleportation races through asteroid fields.

Throughout the years, the ISCP Galactic Campus remained in its isolated corner of the universe, blissfully unaware of the cosmic happenings beyond. And so, the college continued to thrive, fostering a community of oddities and intellectuals, proving that even in the vastness of space, education could be delightfully peculiar.


ISCP Atlantis Campus '1666

In the fabled year of 1666, the International State College of the Philippines Atlantis Campus was founded with great pomp and splendor, or so the ancient scrolls say. Nestled beneath the waves, this underwater institution promised a unique educational experience for merfolk and humans alike.

However, due to a series of unfortunate events involving wayward sea monsters and overly ambitious students attempting to turn kelp into currency, the Atlantis Campus faced constant challenges. Professors, with gills and all, pioneered courses like "Aquatic Alchemy" and "Fishy Philosophy," leaving the students puzzled about the practicality of their newfound knowledge. The campus library, rumored to house the lost city's deepest secrets, turned out to be a collection of soggy scrolls and books nibbled on by mischievous aquatic creatures. Despite these setbacks, the students persevered, mastering the art of bubble-based communication and dolphin-assisted transportation.

Over the centuries, the Atlantis Campus became a symbol of resilience and adaptability, with graduates known for their creativity in solving underwater conundrums. And so, in the depths of the ocean, the International State College of the Philippines Atlantis Campus thrived, proving that even in the mysterious realm of Atlantis, education could be a rather damp affair.
