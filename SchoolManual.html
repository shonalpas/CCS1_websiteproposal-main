THE ISCP SCHOOL MANUAL

Section 1: Attending Classes

1.a Class Attendance: Show up to class whenever you feel like it. After all, who needs to learn and participate in discussions? Professors absolutely love a good game of hide-and-seek.

1.b Taking Notes: Don't bother taking notes; just rely on your photographic memory, which we're sure you possess. If not, just make sure to ask for your friend's notes five minutes before the final exam.

Section 2: Studying

2.a Time Management: Procrastinate until the last minute, then pull an all-nighter before every major assignment or exam. Cramming is the true path to academic enlightenment.

2.b Study Environment: Find the loudest and most distracting place to study, like a rock concert or a construction site. It'll sharpen your ability to concentrate under any circumstances.

Section 3: Social Life

3.a Partying: Make sure to attend every party, even if you have a 9 AM class the next day. Your liver can handle it, and your GPA will thank you for the much-needed break.

3.b Social Networking: Spend at least 80% of your class time scrolling through social media. Professors love the sound of your keyboard clicking while they're teaching the latest breakthroughs in their field.

Section 4: Assignments and Essays

4.a Plagiarism: When in doubt, copy and paste. It's the fastest way to get your assignments done. Just make sure to replace the professor's name with yours.

4.b Citations: Who needs proper citations? Just sprinkle a few random websites and hope that nobody notices. If caught, blame it on your pet hamster's attempt at using the keyboard.

Section 5: Office Hours

5.a Professor Meetings: Never, under any circumstances, visit your professors during office hours. They're just there for decoration, right? Your questions can wait until the night before the final.

Section 6: Graduation

6.a Degree Requirements: Wait until the last semester to check if you've fulfilled your degree requirements. If not, hope for a miracle. Maybe the university will introduce "Astral Projection 101" as a mandatory course.

Remember, this the school manual are meant purely for humor and not to reflect the actual objectives of any real educational institution.
